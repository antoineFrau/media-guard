## What it is
Name Calling/Labeling (FR: Dénigrement / étiquetage) involves attaching pejorative terms to discredit a person or group. It is a form of propaganda that seeks to evoke contempt, rejection, and moral condemnation, often with the intent to dehumanize or short-circuit argument. This technique is commonly used in political and media discourse to polarize audiences and simplify complex issues.

## How to recognize it
- **Appeals Evoked:** Contempt, rejection, moral condemnation.
- **Author Intent:** To discredit, dehumanize, or short-circuit argument.
- **Trigger Terms:** In English, common terms include 'enemy,' 'liar,' 'fool,' and 'denier.' In French, these terms are 'ennemi,' 'menteur,' 'idiot,' and 'négationniste.'
- **Contextual Clues:** The use of exaggerated or emotionally charged language to describe individuals or groups, often without substantive evidence or justification.
- **Overlap with Other Techniques:** Name-calling can be part of a broader smear campaign or used alongside negative epithets and stereotypes.

## Examples
- "WHO: Coronavirus emergency is 'Public Enemy Number 1'"
- "The fake news media is the enemy of the people"
- "Every single one is a lying snake"
- "Climate deniers are dangerous fools. These anti-science fanatics threaten our future"
