## What it is
Slogans are short, memorable phrases designed to encapsulate a message and evoke an emotional response. They are often used in political discourse and propaganda to create a sense of belonging, identity, and emotional resonance. Slogans are typically short and punchy, and they may be combined with other techniques such as flag-waving and repetition.

## How to recognize it
Slogans can be recognized by their brevity, memorability, and emotional appeal. They often use simple, repetitive language and may be combined with other techniques such as flag-waving and repetition. Slogans may also be used to encapsulate a complex message into a catchphrase or rallying cry. Recognition cues include:
- **Appeals evoked:** belonging, identity, emotional resonance
- **Author intent:** to encapsulate a message, create a memorable rallying cry, or reduce a complex idea to a catchphrase

## Examples
- ""BUILD THE WALL!" Trump tweeted."
- ""Take back control!""
- ""No justice, no peace.""
- ""Yes we can!""
