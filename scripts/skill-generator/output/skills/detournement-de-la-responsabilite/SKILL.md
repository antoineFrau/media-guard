## What it is
"Détournement de la responsabilité" is a rhetorical manipulation technique that involves shifting blame or responsibility for a problem, action, or outcome onto another person, group, or institution. This technique is often used to avoid accountability, deflect criticism, or manipulate public opinion. It can be employed in political discourse, media narratives, or everyday conversations to influence perceptions and assign fault unfairly.

## How to recognize it
1. **Blame Shifting**: Look for instances where an individual or group is accused of being responsible for a problem or action that they did not cause or were not primarily responsible for.
2. **Scapegoating**: Identify situations where a specific group or individual is singled out as the cause of broader societal issues, often without sufficient evidence or context.
3. **Deflection**: Notice when someone avoids taking responsibility for their actions by pointing the finger at others, even when the evidence suggests they are accountable.
4. **Emotional Language**: Pay attention to the use of emotionally charged language that aims to provoke a strong reaction and reinforce the narrative of blame.
