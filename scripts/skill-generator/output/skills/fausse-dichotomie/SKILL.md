## What it is
False dichotomy, also known as black-and-white thinking, is a rhetorical manipulation technique that presents a situation as having only two possible outcomes or sides, when in reality, there are more options or nuances. This technique is often used to simplify complex issues, polarize debates, or dismiss alternative viewpoints.

## How to recognize it
1. **Binary Language**: Look for language that presents only two options, such as "either/or," "us vs. them," or "with us or against us."
2. **Simplification of Complex Issues**: Identify when a complex issue is reduced to a simple, binary choice.
3. **Dismissal of Alternative Viewpoints**: Notice when alternative viewpoints or nuances are dismissed or ignored in favor of the two presented options.
4. **Emotional Appeal**: Often, false dichotomies are used to evoke strong emotional responses, making it harder to consider other perspectives.

## Examples
- **"Les antifascistes sont bel et bien les fascistes d'aujourd'hui."** (Video: Iizvn8ZGma4) - This statement presents a binary choice between antifascists and fascists, ignoring the nuances and complexities of political ideologies and actions.
- **"La violence d'extrême droite dans notre pays n'existe pas."** (Video: Iizvn8ZGma4) - This statement dismisses the existence of right-wing violence, presenting a false dichotomy where either right-wing violence exists or it does not, ignoring the possibility of varying degrees and contexts.
