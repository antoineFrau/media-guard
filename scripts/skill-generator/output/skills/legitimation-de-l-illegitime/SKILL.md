## What it is
Legitimation of the illegitimate is a framing technique that involves presenting extreme, violent, or unacceptable groups, ideas, or actions as normal, legitimate, or acceptable. This technique is often used to normalize behaviors that would otherwise be condemned, to gain sympathy or support for extremist causes, or to shift public opinion towards accepting previously marginalized or condemned viewpoints.

## How to recognize it
1. **Comparisons**: The technique often involves comparing extremist actions or groups to legitimate ones, such as equating violent extremist actions with peaceful protests or legitimate political movements.
2. **Rhetorical questions**: Phrases that imply acceptance or normalization of extremist views, such as "When did we start considering this group as legitimate?"
3. **Normalization language**: Describing extremist actions or groups using neutral or positive language, making them seem ordinary or acceptable.
4. **Contextual cues**: The technique is often used in political or media discourse to shift public opinion or to justify actions that would otherwise be condemned.
