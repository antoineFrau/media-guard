## What it is
Fausse symmetrie is a manipulation technique that presents two sides of a debate as equivalent or symmetrical, even when they are not. This technique is often used to minimize the gravity of a position or to avoid taking a side. It can be employed in political, media, or social contexts to create a false sense of balance and neutrality.

## How to recognize it
1. **Language of Equivalence**: Look for phrases that suggest an artificial balance between two positions, such as "both sides are equally responsible" or "there are two sides to every story."
2. **Contextual Discrepancy**: Check if the evidence or facts clearly favor one side over the other, despite the presentation of equivalence.
3. **Avoidance of Accountability**: Notice if the technique is used to avoid taking a clear stance or assigning responsibility where it is due.
