## What It Is
The utilization of authority is a rhetorical device that leverages the perceived credibility, expertise, or power of an individual or institution to bolster an argument or persuade an audience. This technique can be effective because people tend to trust and defer to authority figures, making it a powerful tool in media and political discourse.

## How to Recognize It
1. **Invocation of Authority**: Look for phrases that reference authority figures, such as "experts say," "official sources indicate," or "according to [authority figure]."
2. **Lack of Evidence**: The argument may rely heavily on the authority's statement without providing additional evidence or context.
3. **Appeal to Credibility**: The speaker or writer may emphasize the authority's credentials, position, or reputation to lend weight to the argument.
4. **Contextual Absence**: The authority's statement may be taken out of context or presented without full disclosure of the authority's full perspective.
