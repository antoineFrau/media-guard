## What it is
Détournement de l'attention is a rhetorical technique used to divert attention from a significant issue by introducing or shifting to a different topic. This technique is often employed to distract the audience from a controversial or sensitive subject, thereby controlling the narrative and shaping public opinion.

## How to recognize it
1. **Sudden Topic Shift**: Look for abrupt changes in the subject of discussion.
2. **Introduction of New Information**: Identify instances where new, often sensational or emotionally charged information is introduced to shift focus.
3. **Distraction from Controversial Issues**: Notice when a controversial or sensitive topic is quickly moved away from in favor of a less contentious or more emotionally engaging subject.
4. **Repetition of Key Phrases**: Observe the use of repetitive phrases or slogans that serve to divert attention from the original topic.

## Examples
- **Video Example**: In a video discussing political violence and extremist groups, the speaker shifts the focus by stating, "La France insoumise a fait de la jeune garde son bras armé pour semer la terreur et la violence à l'université ou dans la rue." This statement introduces a new topic related to a specific political group, diverting attention from the broader discussion on political violence.
- **Video Example**: Another instance in the same video includes the statement, "Les antifascistes sont bel et bien les fascistes d'aujourd'hui." This shifts the discussion to labeling a group as fascists, thereby diverting attention from the original topic of political violence.
