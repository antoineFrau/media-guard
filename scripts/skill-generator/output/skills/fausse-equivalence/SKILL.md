## What it is
False equivalence is a rhetorical technique that falsely equates two or more things that are not actually equivalent, often to create a misleading impression of balance or to downplay the significance of one side. This technique can be used to mislead audiences by presenting two sides of an argument as equally valid when they are not, or to equate minor issues with major ones to create a false sense of proportionality.

## How to recognize it
1. **Comparative Language**: Look for phrases like "just as," "equally," "both sides," or "the same as" that suggest equivalence.
2. **Disproportionate Comparison**: Identify instances where the comparison is clearly disproportionate or misleading.
3. **Contextual Differences**: Note if the context or circumstances of the compared elements are significantly different.
4. **Minimization of Severity**: Observe if the technique is used to downplay the gravity of one side of the comparison.
