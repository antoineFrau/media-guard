## What it is
Désinformation is a manipulation technique that involves the deliberate spread of false or misleading information to shape public opinion or discredit individuals or groups. This can include direct lies, omissions, or distortions of facts. The goal is often to create a false narrative or to sway public perception in a particular direction.

## How to recognize it
1. **False Information**: Statements that are factually incorrect or misleading.
2. **Omissions**: Leaving out crucial details that change the context or meaning of the information.
3. **Distortions**: Altering facts or events to present a biased or false narrative.
4. **Contextual Clues**: Look for phrases that imply causality or association without evidence, such as "it has been said that" or "it is known that."

## Examples
- "Il a été raconté que ça aurait été un guet-apens tendu par de méchants antifascistes contre de pauvres et gentils jeunes hommes nationalistes qui passaient par là." (video: Iizvn8ZGma4) - This statement spreads false information to create a misleading narrative.
- "La France insoumise a fait de la jeune garde son bras armé." (video: Iizvn8ZGma4) - This is an example of désinformation by linking a group to a political party without providing evidence.
