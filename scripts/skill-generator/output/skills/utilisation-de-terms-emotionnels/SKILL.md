## What it is
Emotional language utilization is a rhetorical technique that involves the use of emotionally charged terms or phrases to influence the audience's opinion or elicit a strong emotional response. This technique is often employed to polarize debates, create a sense of urgency, or manipulate public perception by appealing to emotions rather than logic or facts.

## How to recognize it
1. **Emotionally Charged Words**: Look for words that evoke strong emotions such as fear, anger, or outrage. Examples include "epuration ethnique" (ethnic cleansing), "fascistes" (fascists), or "déportation" (deportation).
2. **Hyperbolic Language**: Identify phrases that exaggerate the situation or use hyperbolic language to create a dramatic effect.
3. **Loaded Phrases**: Recognize phrases that carry a strong emotional connotation and are designed to provoke a specific emotional response.
4. **Contextual Analysis**: Analyze the context in which these terms are used to determine if they are intended to manipulate the audience's emotions.
