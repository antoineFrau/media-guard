## What it is
Fausse causalité is a manipulation technique that involves creating or exaggerating a causal relationship between two events or elements that are not actually connected. This technique can be used to mislead an audience into believing that one event directly caused another, even when there is no evidence to support this claim. It is often employed in political and media discourse to sway public opinion or to justify certain actions.

## How to recognize it
1. **False Linkage**: Look for statements that assert a direct causal relationship between two events without providing sufficient evidence or logical reasoning.
2. **Exaggeration**: Identify instances where the relationship between two events is exaggerated to make it seem more significant than it actually is.
3. **Contextual Clues**: Pay attention to the context in which the claim is made. Often, false causality is used in emotionally charged or politically sensitive discussions.
4. **Lack of Evidence**: Check if the claim is supported by credible evidence or if it relies solely on assertion.
