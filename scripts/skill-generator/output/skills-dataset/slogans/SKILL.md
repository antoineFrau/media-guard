## What it is
Slogans are concise, repetitive phrases used to convey a message in a memorable way. They are designed to evoke an emotional response and simplify complex issues. Slogans are commonly used in political campaigns, advertising, and media to influence public opinion and rally support.

## How to recognize it
Slogans can be identified by their brevity, memorability, and emotional appeal. They often use simple, powerful language and are repeated frequently. Look for phrases that are designed to be catchy and easily remembered, often used to encapsulate a campaign or movement.

## Examples
- "Build the Wall!" (Trump campaign)
- "Make America Great Again" (Trump campaign)
- "Yes We Can" (Obama campaign)
- "La France d'abord" (French political slogan)
- "Ensemble, c'est possible" (French political slogan)
- "La République en marche" (French political slogan)
