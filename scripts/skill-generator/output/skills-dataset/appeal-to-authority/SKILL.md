## What it is
Appeal to Authority (FR: Appel à l'autorité) is a manipulation technique that leverages emotions by referencing authoritative figures or sources to persuade audiences. It uses experts, institutions, or credible-seeming persons to lend weight to a claim. This technique is designed to evoke appeals of credibility, trust, and expertise, with the author's intent being to persuade by association and lend legitimacy.

## How to recognize it
- **Trigger terms:** Look for phrases like "expert," "renowned," "senior officials," and "Nobel laureates" in English, or "expert," "renommé," "officiels supérieurs," and "lauréats du prix Nobel" in French.
- **Appeals evoked:** The technique often evokes appeals of credibility, trust, and expertise.
- **Author intent:** The author's intent is typically to persuade by association and lend legitimacy to a claim.
- **Context:** Check if the authority is relevant to the claim being made. Irrelevant authorities or exaggerated credentials can be red flags.
- **Overlap:** Be aware of overlaps with glittering generalities when the authority is idealized.

## Examples
- "Monsignor Jean-François Lantheaume, who served as first Counsellor of the Nunciature in Washington, confirmed that 'Vigano said the truth. That's all.'"
- "Dr. Smith, a renowned epidemiologist from Harvard, stated that the vaccine is completely safe."
- "According to senior intelligence officials, the threat level has never been higher."
- "Expert Dr. Jane Wilson warns of catastrophe."
- "Renowned economist Paul Krugman explains why inflation is transitory. Nobel laureates agree."
