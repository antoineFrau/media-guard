## What it is
Appeal to Authority (FR: Appel à l'autorité) is a manipulation technique that leverages the perceived credibility of authoritative figures, experts, or institutions to persuade an audience. This technique is often used to lend weight to a claim, making it appear more valid or trustworthy by association with a respected source.

## How to recognize it
1. **Reference to Authority Figures**: Look for mentions of experts, scientists, celebrities, or well-known institutions.
2. **Trigger Phrases**: Identify phrases that suggest an authoritative endorsement, such as "expert says," "studies show," or "trusted source."
3. **Contextual Clues**: Check if the authority figure is relevant to the topic and if their endorsement is genuinely applicable.

## Examples
- "Monsignor Jean-François Lantheaume, who served as first Counsellor of the Nunciature in Washington, confirmed that 'Vigano said the truth. That's all.'"
- "According to renowned climate scientist Dr. Jane Smith, the new policy will significantly reduce carbon emissions."
