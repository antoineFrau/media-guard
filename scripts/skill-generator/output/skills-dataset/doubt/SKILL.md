## What it is
Doubt is a manipulation technique that questions or casts doubt on the credibility of a person, institution, or claim to undermine their position. It is distinct from legitimate skepticism as it often lacks evidence and aims to sow confusion. Doubt can evoke skepticism, distrust, and uncertainty to paralyze criticism or create false equivalence.

## How to recognize it
Doubt can be recognized by phrases that question credibility without providing evidence, such as "can we really trust" or "so-called experts." It often appears in contexts where the author's intent is to undermine credibility, create false equivalence, or paralyze criticism. In French, similar phrases include "peut-on vraiment faire confiance" or "les soi-disant experts." The technique may also evoke appeals of skepticism, distrust, and uncertainty.

## Examples
- "Can the same be said for the Obama Administration?"
- "But can we really trust these so-called experts? Who funds their research?"
- "The election was stolen"
- "Skeptics ask: can we trust her funding sources?"
- "The so-called experts might say otherwise"
