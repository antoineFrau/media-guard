## What it is
Whataboutism, straw men, and red herrings are rhetorical techniques used to divert attention from the main issue or to undermine an opponent's argument. Whataboutism deflects criticism by pointing to others' faults, straw men misrepresent an opponent's argument to make it easier to attack, and red herrings introduce irrelevant information to distract from the original topic.

## How to recognize it
- **Whataboutism**: Look for phrases that shift the focus to someone else's faults, such as "But what about..." or "But they did..." in English, and "Mais qu'en est-il de..." or "Mais eux aussi..." in French.
- **Straw Men**: Identify when an argument is misrepresented or exaggerated, often with phrases like "They say..." or "The argument is..." in English, and "Ils disent..." or "L'argument est..." in French.
- **Red Herring**: Notice when irrelevant information is introduced, often with phrases like "But don't forget..." or "But what about the fact that..." in English, and "Mais n'oubliez pas..." or "Mais qu'en est-il du fait que..." in French.

## Examples
- **Whataboutism**: "President Trump —who himself avoided national military service in the 1960's— keeps beating the war drums over North Korea."
- **Straw Men**: "They say we should raise taxes, but that's just a way to control people's lives."
- **Red Herring**: "But don't forget, the economy is doing great, so why are we talking about this issue?"
