## What it is
The **bandwagon-reductio-ad-hitlerum** technique combines two distinct manipulation methods:
1. **Bandwagon**: Claims widespread support to pressure conformity (e.g., "Everyone agrees, so should you").
2. **Reductio ad Hitlerum**: Discredits an argument by comparing it to Hitler or Nazism, often without logical relevance.

## How to recognize it
**Bandwagon cues**:
- Appeals to **belonging** ("Join the majority") or **social proof** ("Polls show 90% support").
- Author intent: **pressure conformity** (e.g., framing dissent as unpopular).
- Watch for **fabricated consensus** (e.g., vague claims like "everyone knows").

**Reductio ad Hitlerum cues**:
- Triggers **moral outrage** or **disgust** ("Just like Goebbels' propaganda").
- Author intent: **discredit by extreme comparison** (e.g., equating policies to Nazi actions).
- Key terms: "Nazi," "Hitler," "Goebbels," "Vichy" (FR/EN).

**Confusions to avoid**:
- Bandwagon: Distinguish genuine popularity from fabricated claims.
- Reductio: Valid analogies (e.g., historical parallels) vs. inappropriate Nazi comparisons.

## Examples
- **Bandwagon**: "Real Americans believe in this. Patriots everywhere support it."
- **Reductio ad Hitlerum**: "Vichy journalism... collaborates like the Vichy government with the Nazis."
