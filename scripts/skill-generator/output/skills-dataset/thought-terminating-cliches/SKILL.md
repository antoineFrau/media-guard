## What it is
Thought-terminating clichés are phrases that halt critical thinking and debate. They are often used to dismiss opposing views or to avoid deeper examination of an issue. These clichés create a sense of finality, implying that no further discussion is necessary.

## How to recognize it
Thought-terminating clichés can be identified by their brevity and finality. They often evoke appeals of closure, making the listener feel that the matter is settled. They may also evoke false consensus, suggesting that any reasonable person would agree. The intent behind these clichés is to stop critical thinking, dismiss objections, or short-circuit debate. Look for phrases that end reflection, such as "it is what it is" or "that's just common sense."

## Examples
- "I do not really see any problems there. Marx is the President."
- "It is what it is"
- "That's just common sense. Any reasonable person would agree"
- "That's the way it's always been. Tradition matters"
