## What it is
Thought-terminating clichés are phrases designed to halt critical thinking and discourage further inquiry. They are often used to dismiss opposing viewpoints or to shut down debate without providing substantive arguments. These clichés are prevalent in political discourse and media, where they serve to control the narrative and limit critical analysis.

## How to recognize it
Thought-terminating clichés can be identified by their brevity and lack of substantive content. They often appear as dismissive statements that do not invite further discussion. Common trigger terms in English include:
- "Just because"
- "It is what it is"
- "That's just the way it is"
- "You can't argue with success"

In French, similar phrases are:
- "C'est comme ça"
- "C'est la vie"
- "Il n'y a pas de discussion"

These phrases are typically used to end conversations or debates rather than to engage in meaningful dialogue.

## Examples
- "I do not really see any problems there. Marx is the President."
- "It is what it is. There's nothing we can do about it."
- "C'est comme ça, et c'est tout."
