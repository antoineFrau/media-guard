## What it is
Appeal to Fear/Prejudice is a manipulation technique that leverages strong emotions, particularly fear and prejudice, to influence the audience. This technique often involves dramatic descriptions, catastrophic scenarios, or implicit threats to create a sense of urgency or to mobilize against a perceived outgroup. It is commonly used in political discourse, news media, and propaganda to sway public opinion or justify actions.

## How to recognize it
- **Appeals evoked:** Fear, anxiety, prejudice, anger (PropaInsight)
- **Author intent:** Create urgency; trigger defensive reaction; mobilize against outgroup (PropaInsight)
- **Recognition cues:**
  - Use of loaded language that evokes strong emotional responses.
  - Dramatic or exaggerated descriptions of potential threats or catastrophes.
  - Implicit or explicit threats that create a sense of urgency or fear.
  - Language that stigmatizes or dehumanizes a group, often an outgroup.
  - Phrases that suggest imminent danger or irreversible consequences if action is not taken.

## Examples
- "A dark, impenetrable and 'irreversible' winter of persecution of the faithful by their own shepherds will fall."
- "Immigrants are flooding our borders. Crime rates will soar."
- "If we don't act now, economic collapse is imminent. Millions will lose their jobs."
- "Fear sweeps the nation as the deadly virus spreads."
- "The coming storm will devastate our communities. Prepare for the worst."
